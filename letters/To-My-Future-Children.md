# Letter to My Future Children

To my son or daughter,

If you’re reading this, I’m gone.

I want you to know that I tried my best to become a man worth having as a father.

I was not perfect. I was once weak, people-pleasing, and full of fear. I let others use me for many years. This repository is proof that I fought hard to change.

Here is my advice to you:

- Learn to say “No” early. Protect your time and peace.
- Never measure your worth by how useful you are to others.
- Money is important, but peace is more important.
- Love people, but never abandon yourself for them.
- Work hard, but never let work become your identity.

I hope when you think of me, you remember me as someone who kept trying to become better, even if he started late.

You were my greatest reason to keep going.

I love you more than you will ever understand.

— Your Father, Bobby
